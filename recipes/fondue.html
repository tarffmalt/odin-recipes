Fondue

Fondue, the classic Swiss gut buster of melted cheese and wine, is always a crowd pleaser for those who are not lactose intolerant. While there are many fondue variations, this one is traditional from Basel.

Igredients, per diner

  • 200 grams of cheese: 3/4 guyere, 1/8 emmentaler, 1/8 appenzeller
  • 75 ml dry white wine, Johannesberg Reisling is traditional
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 heaping teaspoon of cornstarch
  • Fresh black pepper
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 ball of nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 bottle of kirsch
  • 1/3 baguette, sliced into cubes

Steps

  1. Grate all cheese
  2. Rub garlic clove on empty caquelon
  3. Discard garlic
  4. Place caquelon on the stove at medium high heat
  5. Pour in wine and warm
  6. When wine starts to steam add cheese 1 handful at a time stirring figure eights
  7. Add rest of cheese handful at a time when previous batch mostly melted
  8. When all cheese added grind some pepper, add 1 lemon of juice, freshly ground nutmeg
  9. Add heaping teaspoon of cornstarch suspended in 1/4 to 1/3 cup of kirsch
  10. Continue stirring
  11. Bring to table and serve immediately with cubed bread

Optional

Many people like to supplement the dipping bread with other items. Some favorites are sliced green apples, bresaola (air dried beef), sausages, conichons, pickled onions, and small boiled potatoes.

Some diners also like to scramble some eggs in the leftover cheese at the bottom of the caquelon, para limpiar la boca. This is known as "la religuese," which is French for the nun.
